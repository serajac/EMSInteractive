Physiologic Adaptations at Birth

🫁 Lung Fluid Clearance

Adrenaline activates sodium channels and catecholamines trigger lung fluid clearance. About one-third is cleared before delivery; vaginal birth compresses the chest and expels more fluid.

💨 First Breath

Triggered by chemical changes (air exposure) and temperature change. Air entering alveoli causes pulmonary capillaries to dilate, reducing pulmonary pressure and allowing blood to flow into the lungs.

❤️ Circulatory Transition

Foramen ovale begins to close. SpO₂ may remain 70–80% for several minutes — normal appearance of cyanosis. SpO₂ may not reach 90% for up to 10 minutes after birth.

🌡️ Temperature Regulation

Newborns have a larger body surface area, less subcutaneous fat, and immature temperature regulation. Immediate drying and warm coverings are essential. Hypothermia worsens hypoxia.

🧠 Hypoxia Sensitivity

Newborns are extremely sensitive to hypoxia. Prolonged hypoxemia causes significant and irreversible brain injury. Causes include decreased brain blood flow, low oxygen content, and cardiovascular anomalies.

🔄 Ductus Closure

The ductus arteriosus and foramen ovale may take several days to fully close. Failure to close results in congenital defects (PDA, etc.) that can require medical or surgical correction.

Neonatal Resuscitation Algorithm

Birth
Assess: Term? Good tone? Breathing/crying?
↓
Initial Steps
Warm, dry, stimulate, position, clear airway. Complete within 60 seconds (The Golden Minute).
↓
HR < 100?
Start PPV at 40–60 breaths/min with approximately 5 cmH₂O PEEP. Apply pulse oximeter.
↓
15 sec: Reassess
Check HR again at 15 seconds and at 30 seconds of effective ventilation with chest movement.
↓
HR ≤ 60?
Add chest compressions — two-thumb encircling technique preferred. Ratio 3:1 (90 compressions: 30 breaths/min). Continue for 60 seconds before reassessing.
↓
Still ≤ 60?
Administer Epinephrine IV/IO: 0.01–0.03 mg/kg per dose (conc. 0.1 mg/mL). ET route: 0.05–0.1 mg/kg. Consider volume expanders: 10 mL/kg NS if hypovolemia suspected.

ET Intubation Indications

  • Tracheal suctioning of thick meconium required
  • Bag-mask or LMA ventilation is ineffective or prolonged
  • When chest compressions are being performed
  • Special circumstances: congenital diaphragmatic hernia or extremely low birth weight (<1 kg)

LMA is appropriate for term and preterm newborns ≥34 weeks or weighing >1.5 kg. Confirm ET placement via direct visualization, waveform capnography, and prompt HR improvement.

Post-Resuscitation Monitoring

⚠️ ET Tube Complications

Watch for tube migration, dislodgement, or occlusion by mucus/meconium. Signs: decreased chest movement, diminished breath sounds, return of bradycardia.

💨 Pneumothorax

Suspect with unilateral decreased breath sounds, unequal chest expansion, or increased resistance to ventilation. Needle decompression per medical direction.

🧊 Therapeutic Hypothermia

Offered in hospital for newborns >36 weeks with moderate-to-severe hypoxic-ischemic encephalopathy. NOT initiated prehospital — continue monitoring and rapid transport.

🚑 Transport

Maintain temperature (warm ambulance), support ventilation, maintain oxygen levels, cover head. Transport to facility with NICU services. Continuously reassess.
